# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Primary Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Understand requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers**: Report progress and identify blockers
- **DevOps Engineers**: Coordinate on deployment and infrastructure needs

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Primary Interactions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on feature design and technical feasibility
- **UX Designers**: Align on user needs and design priorities
- **Sponsors**: Validate strategic alignment and business outcomes

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Primary Interactions
- **Developers**: Track progress and remove impediments
- **Product Managers**: Align on scope, priorities, and timelines
- **Sponsors**: Escalate issues and report status

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive, user-centered experiences. They conduct research, design interfaces, and validate solutions through testing to ensure the product meets user needs and accessibility standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Establish and maintain design systems and patterns
- Collaborate with product and engineering on feasibility and implementation
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design practices

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Improve user satisfaction and task completion rates
- Reduce friction and support costs through better design

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Design specifications and component documentation

### Primary Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Align on user needs and feature priorities
- **Developers**: Collaborate on implementation feasibility and design handoff
- **Business Analysts**: Share insights on user workflows and pain points

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They analyze requirements, document processes, and ensure solutions align with organizational goals and deliver measurable value.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create functional specifications and user stories
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs
- Support change management and user adoption

### Goals
- Ensure solutions deliver measurable business value
- Reduce ambiguity and rework through clear requirements
- Improve process efficiency and effectiveness

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user stories
- Process flow diagrams and data models
- Stakeholder workshops and requirement reviews

### Primary Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Translate business strategy into detailed requirements
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers**: Align on scope, priorities, and deliverables

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure and automation that enables fast, reliable software delivery. They focus on CI/CD pipelines, monitoring, and operational excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Ensure security, compliance, and disaster recovery capabilities
- Support incident response and root cause analysis

### Goals
- Minimize deployment time and increase deployment frequency
- Achieve high system reliability and uptime
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure and deployment documentation
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Performance and reliability metrics dashboards

### Primary Interactions
- **Developers**: Enable efficient development workflows and production deployments
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on infrastructure needs and deployment schedules
- **Support Lead**: Collaborate on incident response and system health

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Lead manages customer support operations and serves as the voice of the customer. They ensure timely issue resolution, capture feedback, and work with product and engineering to address systemic problems.

### Responsibilities
- Manage support team operations and ticket workflows
- Ensure timely and effective customer issue resolution
- Identify and escalate systemic issues to product and engineering
- Track support metrics and customer satisfaction
- Document known issues and workarounds

### Goals
- Achieve high customer satisfaction and rapid issue resolution
- Reduce repeat issues through root cause elimination
- Improve product quality through feedback loops

### Typical Communication
- Support tickets and escalation reports
- Customer feedback summaries and trend analysis
- Knowledge base articles and support documentation

### Primary Interactions
- **Developers**: Escalate bugs and collaborate on issue diagnosis
- **Product Managers**: Share customer feedback and feature requests
- **DevOps Engineers**: Coordinate on incident response and system issues

---

## Sponsors

### Role Summary
Sponsors provide executive support, funding, and strategic direction for projects. They remove organizational barriers, make critical decisions, and ensure projects align with business strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Provide project funding and resource allocation
- Approve major project decisions and scope changes
- Remove organizational barriers and escalations
- Ensure strategic alignment with business objectives
- Review project progress and outcomes

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver strategic business value
- Maximize return on investment
- Enable project success through adequate support and resources

### Typical Communication
- Executive status reports and steering committee meetings
- Strategic decision memos and approvals
- Escalation resolution and stakeholder alignment

### Primary Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Review progress, approve decisions, and remove blockers
- **Product Managers**: Validate strategic alignment and business outcomes
- **Business Analysts**: Ensure solution meets organizational needs

## Persona Review Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure roles and personas remain accurate and relevant as your team and project evolve:

### Quarterly Review
- [ ] Review each persona's responsibilities against current team practices
- [ ] Validate that goals and success metrics are still relevant
- [ ] Update communication channels and tools if changed
- [ ] Check that primary interactions reflect actual collaboration patterns
- [ ] Identify any new roles or personas that should be added
- [ ] Archive or update personas for roles that no longer exist

### When to Trigger an Ad-Hoc Review
- Team structure or reporting lines change significantly
- New tools or processes are adopted that change workflows
- Feedback indicates confusion about roles or responsibilities
- New project types require different collaboration models
- Post-retrospective insights suggest role clarity issues

### Review Process
1. Gather feedback from team members in each role
2. Update persona documentation based on findings
3. Communicate changes to all stakeholders
4. Update any related documentation or training materials
5. Schedule the next review date
